Time Management

Last month I started a subscription with Safari Books Online, O'Reilly's platform for technology and business learning. The subscription was quite expensive, around $400 a years, but it has loads of good content and I kept discovering useful books and videos on it.
Today I finished reading my first lesson which is Time Management on Safari Books Online, and I want to share what I've learned to manage time!
1) First thing I should do is to clean up my workspace! This is quite unexpected advice right? I never thought a messy workspace would undermine productivitiy THAT MUCH, but out of curiosity, I cleaned up my desk. Turns out my productivity at least doubled! The only things on my desk now are a computer monitor, a mouse, a keyboard, a notebook, a speaker and a water bottle!
2) Second thing I learned is to identify the most important tasks! And to "major in the majors"! I have a habit of starting on something very enjoyable in the morning (read a book, play a game, chit chat with people, or gym!). The result of this habit is that I never get to spend enough time with the important task I actually need to do (work on my website, develop a game, etc). So I should give myself a sufficient incentive to do the most important task first! To align with this task, I figured that my current goals are to learn UE4 (a game engine for VR development) and to prepare for coding interviews (so I can apply to other jobs later)!
3) Third point is to control interruptions! This is the hardest thing for me to do. It is hard for me to SAY NO if someone asks me to do something. Squido says always remember to put your own interests before anyone else's. I think this advice goes along here. Whenver someone wants to interrupt me with the task I'm working on, I should feel comfortable to tell him/ her that "I would love to do XXX with you but I have to get back to work because I need to get this done by XXX time". I didn't do this today. When Micky asked me to play game with him, I sat with him even though all the time I felt uncomfortable coz I really should do something more productive. Tomorrow I will be better at this and prioritize my needs first!
That's the three points I learned! I'm so happy today I finished reading this book so fast and let's see where it will take me to :P!
