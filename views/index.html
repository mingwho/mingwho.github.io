# NAMSHI (this is kinda techy?)
Today I'm really really happy because I finished 90% of a task that was assigned to me this week! My task is to enable customers to update their language preference. Basically, Namshi sends out lots of emails and SMS everyday for marketing and notification reasons. And the app decides on the users' preferred language when they purchase an item. However, some users are receiving messages in antoher language that they don't want.
For this task, I wrote both the back-end and the front-end during past few days. The funny thing is that I always think that back-end takes lots of thought and meticulous tuning and the front-end should just easy peasy lemon squicy. However I was very wrong :P
I spent one day writing and testing the API and two days writing the Angular front-end. I have to admit Angular is the most confusing thing I have ever encountered in my coding life... because there are just so many things going on here and there and here and there and ALL OVER THE PLACE... Like mind-blowingly complicated. For example, to make a simple change like this one, I edited 9 files in the front end while in back end I only changed 1 :)
Before I questioned why my boss (Alex) wanted to put me in the back-end team. Now I get it. Back-end is so much less painful. I'm still happy that I tried to write front-end this time. Feeling some super power right now haha
